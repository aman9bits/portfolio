Intent Score ML Model

Decay-weighted model predicting job-seeking intent across ~1M active candidates/month. Improved intent identification accuracy from 40% to 90%.

Signals used: Last login date, profile update recency, job application date, job search activity, CV update date — each with exponential decay (100 → 20 over 6 months) so recent activity is weighted proportionally.

Design choice: Exponential decay was chosen over linear decay to reflect the real-world signal — a login 6 months ago is nearly meaningless; one 3 days ago is highly significant.

Job Ingestion AI Agent — personal productivity tool

An automated agent that monitors job board emails from LinkedIn, Indeed, and Naukri — extracts individual job listings, assesses fit against a PM profile, and logs structured rows into a Google Sheet for triage.

Trigger

New email arrives from job board (LinkedIn, Indeed, Naukri). Gmail label triggers Make.com workflow.

Processing

Apps Script parses email body, identifies multiple jobs per email, checks each against profile criteria via Claude API.

Output

Structured row added to Google Sheets — job title, company, fit score, location, key requirements extracted.

How I work

Product philosophy

Three principles that show up consistently across the work above.

01

Diagnose before building

Every product I've shipped started with rigorous problem framing — recruiter interviews, competitive benchmarking, behavioural data analysis. The build is always faster when the diagnosis is correct.

02

AI as mechanism, not feature

The most effective AI I've shipped was baked into the product's core logic — categorisation, intent prediction, conversational advisory. Not an AI tab. Not a chatbot bolted on. AI as the engine underneath.

03

Measure what actually matters

At Shine, I redefined the north star from revenue to lineup efficiency — a metric that actually told us whether recruiters were getting value. Picking the right metric is a product decision, not a reporting one.
